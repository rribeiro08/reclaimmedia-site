The stories behind Static in the Dark

Understanding the Album

"Static in the Dark" is a concept album about the chaos of your twenties: the breakups, the late nights, the existential dread, and eventually, learning to let go. Each song builds on the last, taking you through the emotional journey of losing yourself and finding your way back.

1. Midnight Thoughts

The album opener about late-night overthinking and the ghosts of relationships past.

2. Paper Walls

A metaphor for relationships built on fragile foundations and hollow promises.

3. Exit Sign

Watching someone leave like they'd been planning it all along, and reclaiming yourself.

4. Somewhere Between

The purgatory of modern dating: situationships and emotional limbo.

5. Twenty-Something

The universal experience of feeling behind while watching everyone else succeed.

6. Almost Called

The small victory of not reaching out when you know you shouldn't.

7. Static

When communication breaks down completely and you lose the frequency you shared.

8. Better Stranger

The bittersweet realization that you're better off as strangers than as lovers.

9. Hometown Ghost

Being haunted by the places that made you and the memories you can't escape.

10. Learn to Leave

The album closer about finally learning to walk away and start again.
