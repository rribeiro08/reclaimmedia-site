What Is "Twenty-Something" About?

"Twenty-Something" is the quarter-life crisis anthem you didn't know you needed. It's about the gap between what you thought your twenties would look like and the chaotic reality. The degree collecting dust. The car that barely runs. The job you hate but can't leave.

But more than that, it's about the pressure of watching everyone else seem to have it together. Friends getting married, buying houses, having kids. While you're eating cereal at midnight wondering what your purpose is. It's the comparison trap that social media makes impossible to escape.

Key Themes

  • Comparison Culture: "Scrolling through their perfect lives"
  • Imposter Syndrome: "We're all just faking it somehow"
  • Unmet Expectations: "They said by now I'd have it made"
  • Collective Solidarity: Realizing no one really has it figured out
