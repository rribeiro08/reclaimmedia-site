What Is "Paper Walls" About?

"Paper Walls" uses the extended metaphor of a house to represent a relationship. The narrator and their partner "built this house from promises," decorating rooms they'd never use and filling closets with excuses. It's about the realization that what looked solid from the outside was actually paper thin.

The song captures the moment after the collapse. The foundation cracked before the paint could fade. Everything they built together was vulnerable to "one breath of truth."

Key Themes

  • False Security: How quickly something that felt permanent can fall apart
  • Hollow Promises: "Forever" said without meaning
  • Aftermath: The toothbrush, the curtains closed, living in the wreckage
  • Healing: The final chorus shifts to "this is where the healing starts"

Musical Approach

The upbeat tempo contrasts with the lyrics, a signature of Colby's style. You might find yourself nodding along before the words hit you. It's the kind of song you can cry to and dance to at the same time.
